GSM Signaling Platform:
International Mobile Subscriber Identity Translation Application

Overview

The GSM Signaling Platform provides SS7 signal processing and subscriber identification translation that enables larger GSM carriers to offer wholesale roaming services to smaller or emerging GSM wireless carriers, who may otherwise not be able to offer extensive roaming coverage to their subscribers. Large carriers can also take advantage of this platform to provide similar roaming capabilities to affiliate companies in different global markets.

Subscribers in a GSM network have an International Mobile Subscriber Identity (IMSI) that identifies them in their carrier's network. In order for the subscribers to roam outside of their home network their carrier needs to negotiate roaming agreements with other network operator(s) so that the visited network recognizes the subscriber's IMSI and allows roaming to proceed. Negotiating and administering a large number of these agreements can be a lengthy and time-consuming process, and there may be a limit on how many roaming agreements the visiting carrier may choose to support. Consequently, new or smaller wireless carriers may have difficulty offering extensive GSM roaming coverage.

A larger GSM carrier may be able to offer roaming capabilities to smaller carriers, or even to smaller divisions within their own company, by acting as a broker. To set-up this service, the broker carrier or parent company utilizes the GSM Signaling Platform in conjunction with their existing roaming agreements and network infrastructure, and then establishes a billing mechanism (transaction or usage-based) to charge the smaller carrier-customer, if necessary.

With a one time 'set it and forget it' agreement with the larger carrier, the carrier-customer or subsidiary gains access to as many visitor networks as the larger carrier offers, without the need to negotiate individual agreements or integrate their SS7 networks.

How Does the GSM Signaling Platform Work?

The larger carrier or broker provides the smaller carrier with a second range of subscriber IMSIs. The larger carrier’s IMSI (the second) is stored in the subscribers mobile phone along with the original IMSI that is provided by the smaller carrier. When the subscriber roams into another network, the larger carrier’s IMSI is activated because the smaller’s IMSI is not recognized. This second IMSI now identifies the roaming customer as the larger carrier’s subscriber. This results in the IMSI query being sent to the GSM Signaling platform (located in the larger carrier’s network) where it is translated (mapped) to the smaller carrier’s IMSI then sent to be validated by the small carrier’s HLR. The translation application is referred to as Dual International Mobile Subscriber Identity (DIMSI) Mapping.

Benefits

New Revenue Potential for Existing GSM Carriers
The GSM Signaling Platform allows the roaming broker to generate new revenue from other GSM carriers. The platform, combined with the broker’s extensive roaming coverage and billing system, allows the broker to offer a 'set it and forget it' roaming service that reduces lengthy negotiations and administration hassles for their carrier-customer. The application is also ideal for larger carrier’s wanting to supply roaming to smaller subsidiaries within their company.

Cost Savings and Speed to Market
By using the signaling platform, larger carriers can provide GSM roaming capabilities to affiliate providers who may otherwise have not been able to negotiate these services on their own. Not only does this give them a big boost competitively, it also reduces the expense the larger carrier could incur in establishing and maintaining centralized databases for these smaller operating companies.

Carrier-Grade Reliability
When deploying platforms in a mated pair configuration, with each link fully redundant, each signaling device is capable of handling the full load of message traffic should the other go out of service, ensuring reliable operation of the system as a whole. The GSM Signaling Platform provides superior reliability, comparable to fault-tolerant "five nines" systems.

CompactPCI® Form Factor
One of the inherent benefits of the CompactPCI® form factor is the ability to easily "hot-swap" I/O units and quickly get back into service in the event of hardware failure, without the need to power down the system. Faster replacement and minimal server outage impact make CompactPCI the choice of carriers seeking high reliability solutions.

Distributed Software Architecture
With distributed software architecture, two or more systems are connected to each other using redundant Ethernet or serial high-speed connections. This architecture distributes the Performance Technologies SS7 software stack across multiple machines, unlike traditional stacks that run on a single computer, or even on a single processor. This feature allows system and SS7 link load sharing among platform units, with platform clusters capable of acting as a single point code. Since the signaling traffic is constantly distributed among units, any system or network failure is handled with no switchover delay. Two or more units in a cluster configuration provide reliability comparable to fault-tolerant "five nines" systems with no single point of failure. With the distributed architecture, each platform unit will automatically take on the control and load balancing function of the entire configuration when called upon to do so.

Network-Proven Redundant Solutions
Performance Technologies signaling solutions have been deployed by OEMs and international and domestic carriers in wireless and wireline configurations all over the world, including the United States, Canada, France, UK, Netherlands, Brazil, Mexico, Japan, and China. Our signaling platform contains multiple co-resident stacks conforming to ANSI, ITU-T, Japan TTC or NTT, and Chinese SS7 specifications, allowing customers to transparently deploy their own IP network solution throughout the world without the need to rewrite their own software code. The GSM Signaling Platform supports V.35, T1 and E1 interfaces for the connection of SS7 links.

Exceptionally Scalable
The GSM Signaling Platform provides the flexibility to cost-effectively grow in capacity and functionality. Customers can begin with a modest single or dual platform deployment and incrementally add units as traffic grows and increased reliability is needed. In distributed mode, the GSM Signaling Platform provides reliable configurations supporting up to 96 SS7 links.

Easily Configurable
With its enhanced STP capabilities, the GSM Signaling Platform can also emulate the presence of multiple end points on the SS7 network, effectively providing access to more voice trunks and more signaling links, without violating SS7 network provisioning guidelines.

Features 

Flexible and Reliable Performance Technologies Distributed SS7 Software
The GSM Signaling Platform utilizes the same distributed SS7 software architecture found in Performance Technologies’ network proven signaling gateway offerings. This feature creates a more scalable architecture, inherent high reliability and greater flexibility for SS7 network interworking.

GSM MAP, CAP and TCAP Validation
The GSM Signaling Platform validates and manipulates messages that contain TCAP, GSM MAP (versions 1, 2, 2+), and GSM CAP (version 2+) data. Modifications to protocol format support are achieved by upgrading database files rather than requiring software modifications. Performance Technologies strictly adheres to industry accepted standards promoted by the GSM MoU Association and International Telecommunication Union (ITU).

Sophisticated Global Title Translation
The GSM Signaling Platform performs full or partial Global Title Translation (GTT) to determine the proper routing of each SS7 message.

Compact Footprint
The GSM Signaling Platform is extremely compact, utilizing hardware units that, when combined, take up less than 8" in a standard telecom rack in non-distributed mode. With the capacity to handle up to 24 SS7 links in a single 3U (5 1/4 " high) unit, several units can support large SS7 configurations that consume far less space than traditional SS7 hardware solutions. The signaling platform takes up less than 24" of vertical rack space when supporting 96 links in distributed (high availability) mode.

Dual Ethernet
The GSM Signaling Platform provides redundant 10/100Base-T Ethernet access, using either a single IP address for easy-to-implement reliability or by using two different IP addresses for enhanced reliability.

Network Management
The SS7 network requirements of the GSM Signaling Platform can be configured remotely using a web-based interface. The platform provides logs and alarms for integrated SS7 network management, and support for SNMP.
